## Pre-Ride Safety Checks

Before you even get on your horse, it’s crucial to perform thorough safety checks:

1. **Equipment Inspection**:

   - Ensure the saddle is clean and in good condition.
   - Check that all straps and buckles are secure.

2. **Horse Health Check**:

   - Examine the horse for any signs of injury or discomfort.
   - Make sure hooves are clean and free of debris.

3. **Environment Assessment**:
   - Look for potential hazards in the riding area.
   - Ensure the space is secure and there are no distractions.

[¹]: Performing these checks helps prevent accidents and reassures both the horse and rider.

## Safe Riding Practices

Maintaining safety while riding is essential. Here are key practices to follow:

| **Practice**      | **Guideline**                                                   |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Wear Safety Gear  | Always wear a helmet, gloves, and appropriate footwear.         |
| Maintain Control  | Use gentle rein pressure and be aware of your horse's response. |
| Ride with a Buddy | Whenever possible, ride with someone else for added safety.     |

### Additional Guidelines:

- Keep your legs relaxed to avoid spooking the horse.
- Use commands clearly and consistently to communicate effectively with your horse.

## Emergency Preparedness

Being prepared for emergencies can make all the difference:

- **Know Your Emergency Contacts**:

  - Keep a list of contacts for local veterinarians or emergency services.

- **First-Aid Kit**:

  - Assemble a first-aid kit with essentials for both horse and rider.

- **Emergency Plan**:
  - Discuss and practice what to do in case of accidents. Make sure everyone involved knows the plan.

> **Tip**: Regularly review your emergency procedures with your riding group to keep everyone informed and prepared.
